Responding to Alarms

P2000 Alarm Manager allows you to enter one or more text-based responses to record noteworthy information associated with a particular alarm. For example, a guard can enter a response explaining that he had to call 911 as a result of an alarm because he believed an armed intruder had breached security.

Under certain conditions, you can enter an alarm response when an alarm status is Pending or Acknowledged. You can also enter a response when the alarm status is Responding. See Alarm Life Cycle for detailed information.

To respond to an alarm:

1.     Locate the alarm to which you wish to respond.

2.     Enter a response in the Add a note text box. As you type, all text matching predefined responses appear. If the software does not find a match, the list of predefined responses does not appear. You may select a predefined response or continue entering your response manually.

For information on how to manage the predefined list of responses, refer to the P2000 Software User Manual.

Note:    To cancel the response, delete the text from the text box.

3.     Click the plus sign (+) at the end of the text box.

The P2000 Alarm Manager records the response in the alarm history.
